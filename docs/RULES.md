# THE SINGULARITY — Rules Reference (Base Game)

**2–4 Players | 30–60 Minutes | Ages 14+**

## Introduction

In The Singularity, you guide the development of artificial intelligence across ten eras of technological evolution. Beginning with simple mechanical calculators and culminating in post-singularity transcendence, you will Research new technologies, Deploy them to your tech stack, Execute their abilities, and race to achieve critical Breakthroughs before your opponents.

Technologies you deploy will grant you resources, trigger powerful effects, and sometimes force your opponents to comply with your overrides. But be careful: the technologies you share may empower your rivals, and the race toward the Singularity is unpredictable.

## Components

- **105 Technology Cards** across 10 Eras (10 per era for Eras 1–9, 15 for Era 10)
- **5 Domains** (colors): Processing (blue), Labor (red), Ethics (green), Creativity (purple), Connection (yellow)
- **6 Resource Icons**: Circuit, Neural Net, Data, Algorithm, Human Mind, Robot
- Standard Breakthrough tokens (achievements) for each era
- Special Breakthrough tokens (triggered by specific card effects)

## Card Anatomy

- **Era Number**: Shown in the center, indicating technological era (1–10)
- **Domain Color**: Background color indicates domain
- **Card Name**: The technology, algorithm, or concept
- **Resource Icons**: Up to four icons in corners (top-left, bottom-left, bottom-right, top-right). The fourth position may be blank
- **Dogma Effects**: One or more special abilities that activate when Executed

## Setup

1. Separate cards into ten supply piles by Era (1–10). Shuffle each face down.
2. Place Standard Breakthrough tokens in a row, one per Era (1–9). Set Special Breakthrough tokens nearby.
3. Each player draws two cards from Era 1.
4. Each player simultaneously chooses one card to Deploy face up, placing the other in hand.
5. The player whose deployed card name comes first alphabetically takes the first turn.

## Key Concepts

### Tech Stack (Board)
Where you Deploy Technology Cards. Organized into up to five stacks, one per Domain. Only the top card is fully active, but Proliferating reveals icons on lower cards.

### Archive (Score Pile)
Face-down pile of Scoreed cards. The total of Era numbers is your score.

### Hand
Cards you have Researched but not yet Deployed. Hidden from other players.

### Supply Piles
Ten face-down piles, one per Era. When you Research, you draw from these. If a pile is empty, draw from the next higher Era.

### Resource Icons
The six icons (Circuit, Neural Net, Data, Algorithm, Human Mind, Robot) are the currency of interaction. When Executing Override effects, you compare icon counts with opponents.

## Turn Structure

On your turn, take **two actions** (starting player takes only one on the very first turn).

### Research (Draw)
Draw one card from the supply pile matching the highest Era on your tech stack. If empty, draw from next higher Era.

### Deploy (Meld)
Place a card from hand on top of the matching Domain's stack. If no stack of that Domain exists, start a new one.

### Execute (Dogma)
Activate a top card's dogma effects. Two types:

- **Override Effects**: Text beginning with "I Override" forces every opponent with fewer of the featured icon to carry out the effect (clockwise). Opponents with equal or more are immune.
- **Cooperative Effects**: All other dogma text. Each opponent with at least as many of the featured icon executes first (clockwise), then you. If any opponent shared, you get one free Research afterward.

Execute all effects in order (overrides first, then cooperative).

### Achieve a Breakthrough
Claim one Standard Breakthrough if: your Archive score >= 5x the Era number, AND you have a top card with Era >= the Breakthrough's Era. Card and score are not spent.

## Proliferating (Splaying)

Some effects instruct you to Proliferate a stack in a direction:

- **Left**: Reveals bottom-right and top-right icons on underlying cards
- **Right**: Reveals top-left and bottom-left icons on underlying cards
- **Up**: Reveals bottom-left, bottom-right, and top-right icons (most powerful)

Requires 2+ cards. Stays until changed. Up supersedes left/right.

## Actions Within Dogma Effects

- **Archive (Tuck)**: Place card at bottom of a stack
- **Score (Score)**: Move card to your Archive
- **Recall (Return)**: Send card back to its Era's supply pile
- **Research**: Draw a card as specified
- **Deploy**: Play a card as specified

## Winning the Game

### Standard Victory

| Players | Breakthroughs to Win |
|---------|---------------------|
| 2       | 6                   |
| 3       | 5                   |
| 4       | 4                   |

### Special Victory
Certain late-Era cards provide alternative win conditions that trigger immediately.

### Supply Exhaustion
If no supply pile has cards when you need to draw, the game ends. Highest Archive score wins (tiebreak: most Breakthroughs).

## Important Rules

- Draw from empty pile -> draw from next higher Era. No higher piles -> game ends.
- You may Deploy a lower Era card on top of a higher one.
- Count every visible icon on your entire tech stack, including Proliferated icons.
- Override and cooperative effects are mandatory.
- "You may" = optional. Otherwise, effects are mandatory.
- Scoreed cards are face down. Anyone can count cards in any Archive but cannot look at them.
- Single-card stacks cannot be Proliferated.
- Effects resolve top to bottom as written on the card.

## Quick Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Archive | Score pile (sum of Era numbers = score) |
| Breakthrough | Achievement token (score + era requirements) |
| Deploy | Play card from hand to tech stack |
| Domain | Card color/category (Processing, Labor, Ethics, Creativity, Connection) |
| Era | Card's technological period (1-10) |
| Execute | Activate a deployed card's dogma effects |
| Score | Move card to Archive (score pile) |
| Proliferate | Fan out a stack to reveal icons |
| Recall | Return card to its Era's supply pile |
| Research | Draw card from supply pile |
| Tech Stack | Board of deployed cards, organized by Domain |
